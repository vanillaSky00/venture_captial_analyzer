## Executive Summary (TL;DR)

Areis is Taiwan's only publicly-listed domestic ISO/ESG third-party certification body (CB), occupying the **midstream regulatory gatekeeping node** between international standards bodies and the ~1,852 listed Taiwanese companies now subject to mandatory ESG disclosure, carbon verification, and cybersecurity auditing. It is headquartered in Tainan, operating nationally across five Taiwan offices and one Vietnam subsidiary (Binh Duong), with partner agents in Singapore, India, and Indonesia. Revenue grew at a 45% CAGR from FY111 to FY113 (NT$70.6M → NT$148.8M), driven by a policy-mandated demand wave from the FSC's 2022 sustainability roadmap and MOENV's carbon fee schedule. The primary concern is margin compression — gross margin fell from 56.9% to 54.2% in FY113, with net income declining 4% despite 40% revenue growth, reflecting auditor headcount scaling ahead of productivity. The key diligence question: **Is margin compression temporary (hiring lag) or structural (pricing pressure from SGS/BSI)?** The answer determines whether this is a compounding compounder or a margin-trapped services firm.

## One-Page Investment Narrative

**Why now:** Taiwan's ESG verification market is in a policy-forced growth phase with a legally mandated client expansion timeline through 2029. Areis is the only listed domestic CB and sits at the exact intersection of three converging regulatory mandates (FSC sustainability roadmap, MOENV carbon fee, cybersecurity law). This is not a bet on organic market development — it is a bet on a scheduled pipeline.

**Why Areis specifically:** Among the 9 TAF-accredited CBs, Areis is the only one that is both (a) Taiwan-headquartered with no overseas parent extracting returns and (b) publicly listed with transparent financials. SGS and BSI serve the market but their Taiwan profits flow offshore. Areis is the pure-play, domestically aligned instrument.

**The bear case:** SGS Taiwan's 150+ auditors and 8,000+ issued certificates represent an installed base Areis cannot displace in the premium enterprise segment. If FSC or MOENV regulations allow self-declaration or lower-grade assurance for smaller entities, Areis's organic pipeline shrinks. Margin recovery may not materialize if auditor talent costs keep rising (Taiwan's aging workforce and specialist scarcity).

**The bull case:** Every NT$100M of listed-company sustainability report spending in Taiwan requires a TAF-accredited CB to sign off. With 1,852 listed companies entering mandatory disclosure and only 9 CBs authorized to verify, Areis does not need to win a competitive battle — it needs to absorb its share of a mandated market. At 8–12% market share and a NT$5–10B TAM, Areis's revenue ceiling is NT$400–1,200M — vs. current NT$148M. At 15% net margin (recovered from FY113's 14.5%), that implies a potential NT$60–180M annual net income at maturity.

**Valuation reference:** Risk-adjusted DCF (18% discount, 25% failure probability) suggests NT$270M mid-point enterprise value. Current equity on book is NT$176.7M. At 40% revenue growth and margin recovery, a strategic acquirer from Japan/Korea (where domestic CBs have been acquired by global testing companies) might value Areis at 3–4× revenue = NT$450–600M within 3 years.
